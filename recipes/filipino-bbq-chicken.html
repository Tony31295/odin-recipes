Filipino-Style Barbecue Chicken

Recipe found on allrecipes.com.

As you can tell from the title, this is a Filipino take of barbeque chicken. The main item that makes this barbeque chicken, Filipino, is the inclusion of banana ketchup. Try out the receipe for yourself and see if you like it!

Ingredients

For the Banana Ketchup

  • 1 medium very ripe banana, mashed
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vingear
  • 2 tabllespoons brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons freshly grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/4 cup water

For the Chicken and Marinade

  • 1/2 cup prepared banana ketchup
  • 3/4 cup lemon-lime sode (Such as 7-Up or Sprite)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 4 cloves crushed garlic
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 medium lemon, juiced
  • 2 1/2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs, cut in half

For the Basting Sauce

  • 3 stablespopons reserved banana ketchup
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce

Directions

Steps

  1. Mash rip banana, tomato paste, vinegar, brown sugar, oil, ginger, onion powder, garlic powder, cayenne, salt, all spice, and turmeric together in a saucepan. Add water and stir to combine
  2. Place over medium-high heat and bring to a simmer. Stir to combine. Reduce heat to medium and simmer until reduced and thickened , 7 to 10 minutes. Remove from heat; reserve 3 tablespoons for the basting sauce, leaving about 1/2 cup for the marinade
  3. Transfer 1/2 cup banana ketchup to a bowl and add lemon-lime soda, soysasuce, garlic, brown sugar, black pepper, and lemon juice for the marinade. Whisk until combined. Add halved chicken thighs and toss to coat.
  4. Cover with plastic wrap and marinate in the refrigerator for 4 to 18 hours
  5. When you are ready to cook the chicken, combine reserved 3 tablespoons banana ketchup, soy sauce, brown suar, oil, and fish suace in a small bowl for basting sauce
  6. Preheat a charcoal grill until coals are very hot
  7. Remove chicken from the refrigerator and place on metal skewers, folding in half with the smooth side on the outside, the rougher side folded in.
  8. Grill chicken over hot coals until no longer pink in the centers, 15 to 20 minutes, turning and basting every 2 to 3 minutes. Serve with accumulated juices or extra basting sauce.
